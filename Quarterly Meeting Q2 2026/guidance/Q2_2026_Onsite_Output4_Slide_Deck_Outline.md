# Output 4: Slide Deck Outline

The deck supports the agenda. It does not replace conversation. Most slides are shown briefly (1-2 min) to anchor a session, then the facilitator moves to discussion. Slides marked "Essential" must be in the deck. Slides marked "Optional" can be cut if time compresses.

## Day 1 Session Slides

### Slide 5: Retrospective Prompt
- **Title:** Evidence of Progress Since Q4 Onsite
- **Purpose:** Anchor the retrospective.
- **Key message:** "Share 2-3 wins with supporting evidence. What changed? What's the proof?"
- **Visual:** Three prompt boxes: "What did we achieve?" / "What improved?" / "What do we know now that we didn't then?"
- **Essential.**

### Slide 6: Q425 Success Criteria Recap
- **Title:** What We Committed to in Q4 2025
- **Purpose:** Reference during the Q425 adoption check session.
- **Key message:** The five Q425 success criteria:
  1. Operating Model v1.0 in Teams
  2. Scorecard v1.0 with owners
  3. GAIN scripts adopted
  4. Architecture contribution loop
  5. 30-day AI experiments
- **Visual:** Checklist format. Grading happens live (checkmarks, partial, or X).
- **Essential.**

### Slide 7: Continue / Start / Stop Categories
- **Title:** Continue / Start / Stop
- **Purpose:** Frame the exercise scope.
- **Key message:** Focus on leader behaviors, operating habits, communication patterns, prioritization, focus protection, modernization behaviors, accountability norms, AI adoption practices, architectural and delivery discipline.
- **Visual:** Three columns (Continue / Start / Stop) with the scope categories listed below.
- **Essential.**

### Slide 8: Personal Vision and Goals Introduction
- **Title:** Your Vision, Your Roles, Your Goals
- **Purpose:** Introduce the BYB framework.
- **Key message:** "You will either lead a life by design or live a life by default."
- **Visual:** The BYB template structure: Role > Vision > Goals. Show the example page from the folder.
- **Essential.**

### Slide 9: Personal Vision Prompts
- **Title:** Drafting Your Vision
- **Purpose:** Guide the silent writing session.
- **Key message:** Five prompts:
  - What is your vision for your leadership role for the remainder of 2026?
  - What are 2-3 goals for the next quarter?
  - How does your vision reflect your character and core values?
  - How does it align with and contribute to collective goals?
  - Where is the gap between your intent and your current behavior?
- **Visual:** Numbered prompt list. Clean, readable.
- **Essential.**

## Day 2 Session Slides

### Slide 10: Day 1 Recap
- **Title:** What We Built Yesterday
- **Purpose:** Re-ground Day 2 in Day 1 outputs.
- **Key message:** Summary of key Day 1 outputs (filled in after Day 1).
- **Visual:** Photo of the wall / boards from Day 1. Or a simple bullet summary.
- **Essential.**

### Slide 11: Team Goals Brainstorm
- **Title:** What Should We Achieve Together?
- **Purpose:** Launch the whiteboard goals exercise.
- **Key message:** "Goals, aspirations, objectives, and outcomes for Q3 and H2 2026. No filter. Volume first."
- **Visual:** Open whiteboard image or blank grid. Minimal text.
- **Essential.**

### Slide 12: Goal Refinement Questions
- **Title:** Making Commitments Real
- **Purpose:** Guide Phase 3 of the whiteboard exercise (the hidden SMART conversion).
- **Key message:** Five questions:
  - How would we know if we achieved this?
  - Who owns it?
  - By when?
  - Is it within our control?
  - Is it specific enough to act on tomorrow?
- **Visual:** Five question cards. Do NOT label them S-M-A-R-T.
- **Essential.**

### Slide 13: AI Adoption Status
- **Title:** AI Adoption: Where We Are
- **Purpose:** Frame the AI governance discussion.
- **Key message:** Current state: tools in use, experiments run, cost data, barriers encountered. (Fill in with actual data from the status round.)
- **Visual:** Table or grid showing team / tool / experiment / status.
- **Optional.** (Can be run as pure discussion without slides.)

### Slide 14: Carry the Load
- **Title:** Carry the Load: Team First
- **Purpose:** Transition to the outdoor exercise.
- **Key message:** Rules: all weight finishes together, no one carries the same load the whole way, notice before being asked, team pace set by the slowest, finish together.
- **Visual:** Simple, clean. Minimal text. Maybe an image of the route or the weighted items.
- **Essential.**

### Slide 15: Commitment Template
- **Title:** Your Commitment
- **Purpose:** Frame the closing commitments.
- **Key message:** Three prompts:
  - My one thing for the next 30 days:
  - One behavior I will stop:
  - My accountability partner:
- **Visual:** Card format. Print as physical cards for each person to fill out.
- **Essential.**

### Slide 16: Closing
- **Title:** Sharpen the Standard
- **Purpose:** Close the onsite.
- **Key message:** "Reach that destination where the things that are pleasant and the things that are honorable, finally become, for you, the same." (Seneca)
- **Visual:** Quote on a clean background. No other text.
- **Essential.**

### Slide 17: Follow-Through
- **Title:** What Happens Next
- **Purpose:** Confirm the follow-through mechanism.
- **Key message:**
  - 48 hours: personal vision documents uploaded to Confluence
  - Weekly: 3-bullet metric update from each leader
  - Monthly: scorecard review in SEL meeting
  - Next onsite: revisit vision, goals, and commitments
- **Visual:** Timeline or cadence calendar.
- **Essential.**

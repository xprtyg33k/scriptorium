## Repeated Patterns from Past Quarterlies

**Consistent across all five quarterlies (Q124 through Q125):**
1. Opening wins/retrospective round robin (every quarter)
2. Goal setting exercise (every quarter)
3. Growth/development plans discussion (every quarter)
4. Architecture or modernization topic (every quarter)
5. Focus and prioritization theme (every quarter)
6. Definition of excellence (Q124, Q224, referenced in others)

**Format evolution:**
- Q124: Informal brainstorming list, no time blocks, no explicit outputs
- Q224: More structured with categories, still no time blocks
- Q324: Two-day format with AM/PM splits, audiobook listening, commitments section
- Q125: Two-day (Tue-Wed), themed sections (Growth, Together, Outward), external deep dive, offsite activity
- Q425: Most mature. Design principles stated. Every session has purpose, method, inputs, outputs. Success criteria defined. GAIN framework. Three-tier scorecard.

**What has landed well and should carry forward:**
- Wins retrospective as opener (anchors the room, builds momentum)
- Round-robin participation design (prevents dominant voices from taking over)
- Artifact-producing sessions (scorecard, operating model, commitments)
- Themed structure with a unifying concept
- Afternoon energy management (lighter, more interactive sessions after lunch)
- Offsite/physical activity as a reset

**What should be retired or evolved:**
- Audiobook listening sessions (too passive for a team at this maturity level)
- Identity/who-are-we exercises (done thoroughly in Q124, Q224, Q425; manifestos exist)
- CliftonStrengths speed-pairing (done in Q425; diminishing returns)
- Communication styles primer from scratch (done in Q425 with "Surrounded by Idiots")
- Operating Model full walkthrough (v1.0 exists from Q425; review adoption, don't rebuild)
- "Get Fired Ideas" and similar gamified brainstorming (this team needs execution discipline, not more idea generation)

- Quarterly dedicated sprints proposed (Jan 2025) but adoption unclear
- Signal R memory leaks requiring nightly reboots

**3. IT infrastructure friction (chronic, worsening)**
- VDI provisioning blocks onboarding for weeks

- Scott Seely appears in nearly every weekly meeting as the primary builder and demonstrator
- Individual heroics celebrated (overnight autonomous agent builds) while systemic capability gaps persist
- Guillermo's review: "We will not confuse heroics with health"

**5. Focus protection and context switching**
- Raised in Q124 ("merciless prioritization"), Q224 ("essential few vs. trivial many"), Q324 (The ONE Thing), Q425 ("saying no without saying no")
- Still a live issue: unplanned work crowds out proactive work

**6. AI adoption fragmentation**
- No standardized tooling across teams
- Each team uses different tools (Claude Code, Copilot, Augment, Kiro)

## Analytical Requirements: Answers

**1. What themes are persistent across multiple quarters or documents?**
Focus/prioritization, outcomes vs. output, metrics discipline, tech debt, architecture modernization, growth/development, accountability, definition of excellence.

**2. What appears unresolved or repeatedly problematic?**
Metrics reliability (JIRA data quality, gaming), tech debt velocity, IT friction, hero culture, focus protection, AI tooling standardization.

**3. What has changed recently enough that Q2 2026 must adjust?**
AI CoE established, Rafael joined, Platform team formalized, 2026 OKRs quantified, Guillermo's performance review made "operationalize" explicit, Q425 artifacts exist as a new baseline, "first team" concept introduced.

**4. Where is the strongest need for conversation versus presentation?**
Conversation: metrics accountability, AI governance, tech debt trade-offs, personal vision alignment, continue/start/stop. Presentation (brief): Q425 artifact adoption status, 2026 OKR context, security posture update.

**5. What should be measured more explicitly?**
Sprint commitment accuracy, escape defect rate (with agreed methodology), PR cycle time, deployment frequency, tech debt points completed per quarter, AI tool adoption rate, time-to-productivity for new hires/contractors.

**6. Which topics are best handled in plenary versus small-group?**
Plenary: retrospective, continue/start/stop harvest, security session, product alignment, commitments. Small-group: SMART goal refinement, AI adoption micro-plans, metrics baseline work.

**7. Which topics need facilitator notes because the visible agenda should stay cleaner?**
Retrospective (hidden evidence standard), whiteboard goals (hidden SMART conversion design), personal vision (gentle push toward gap identification), continue/start/stop (push past safe answers toward real behavioral commitments).

**8. What is the minimum viable agenda if time compresses?**
Retrospective with evidence (60 min), Continue/Start/Stop (45 min), Personal vision and goals (60 min), Commitments round (30 min), Carry the Load outdoor exercise (45 min). Total: ~4 hours. Everything else is valuable but cuttable.

**9. What topics should be cut first if guest timing, travel, or energy degrades?**
Cut first: tech debt deep dive (can be handled async), AI governance details (defer to a follow-up working session), Q425 artifact review (can be done via Confluence pre-read). Cut last: retrospective, personal vision, commitments, Carry the Load.

**10. What follow-through mechanism should exist after the onsite?**
Weekly 3-bullet metric update from each leader, monthly scorecard review in SEL meeting, quarterly revisit of personal vision and SMART goals, Confluence page with all onsite artifacts, 30-day check-in on specific commitments.

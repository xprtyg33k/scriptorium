# Output 2: Recommended 2-Day Agenda

## Theme: Sharpen the Standard

**Dates:** Monday, April 13 and Tuesday, April 14, 2026
**Times:** Mon 8:30 AM to 5:00 PM, dinner at 6:00 PM. Tue 8:30 AM to 3:00 PM.
**Location:** Conference room + breakout spaces + outdoor area nearby
**Guests:** Dan (Director of Information Security) and Clarissa (Chief Product Officer) join **Tuesday morning**.

### Design Principles

- Every session produces an artifact or a decision, not just discussion.
- Morning energy goes to the hardest thinking. Afternoons are interactive and collaborative.
- Monday is the team's own day: retrospective, accountability, personal vision, leadership behaviors. No guests.
- Tuesday morning absorbs guests naturally into alignment sessions, then closes with goals, outdoor exercise, and commitments.
- Q425 produced an Operating Model v1.0, Scorecard v1.0, and GAIN framework. This onsite checks adoption and sharpens, not rebuilds.
- The theme "Sharpen the Standard" signals: we have established what good looks like. Now we make it cut.

### 12:45 - 1:45 | Whiteboard Goals: From Aspiration to Commitment

**Purpose:** Generate team-level goals and outcomes, then refine into measurable commitments. Required exercise with hidden SMART conversion.

**Format:**
- Phase 1 (15 min): Open brainstorm. Write goals/aspirations/outcomes on whiteboard. No filter.
- Phase 2 (10 min): Cluster and prioritize. Vote on top 8-10.
- Phase 3 (20 min): Refinement. For each top item, facilitator asks:
  - "How would we know if we achieved this?" (Measurable)
  - "Who owns it?" (Assignable)
  - "By when?" (Time-bound)
  - "Is this within our control?" (Realistic)
  - "Specific enough to act on tomorrow?" (Specific)
- Phase 4 (15 min): Finalize clean list with owners and deadlines.

**Visible agenda:** "Team Goals and Commitments for Q3 and H2 2026." The word SMART does not appear.

**Output:** 8-10 team-level goals with owners and deadlines.

**Who facilitates:** Guillermo leads Phases 1-2. Different leader facilitates Phases 3-4.

**Energy:** Medium-high. Interactive. Physical (standing at whiteboard).

**Key message:** "We will not confuse activity with value. We will not confuse opinions with evidence."

---

### 1:45 - 2:30 | Carry the Load: Team First (Outdoor Exercise)

**Purpose:** Physical and mental reset. Reflection on shared burden and leadership responsibility. Required exercise.

**Format:**
- Weighted items (sandbags, loaded packs) provided. More weight than one person can carry alone.
- Team walks a defined route together (20-25 min).
- Rules: all weight finishes together, no one carries the same load the whole way, notice and offer before being asked, pace set by the slowest, finish together.
- Seated debrief (10 min):
  - "What did you notice about how the team distributed the load?"
  - "Who noticed when someone was struggling?"
  - "Where in our real work are we carrying load alone that the team should share?"

**Output:** Insight that feeds into closing commitments.

**Title on visible agenda:** "Carry the Load: Team First"

**Energy:** Physical reset. Gets people out of the room.

---

### 2:30 - 3:00 | Commitments, Integration, and Close

**Purpose:** Finalize all artifacts. State explicit commitments. Close with clarity.

**Format:**
- 5 min: Review key artifacts (wins board, C/S/S commitments, Q425 scorecard, SMART goals, AI decisions, guest commitments)
- 15 min: Commitments round robin. Each leader states:
  - Their "one thing" for the next 30 days
  - One behavior they will stop
  - Their accountability partner from this group
- 10 min: Facilitator closes.
  - Grade the three onsite success criteria
  - Name the follow-through mechanism
  - Closing message and final one-word round

**Output:** Documented commitments with owners, deadlines, and accountability partners.

**Key message:** "If an individual's vision does not align with and contribute to collective goals, that is a problem. We leave here aligned. The work is proving it."

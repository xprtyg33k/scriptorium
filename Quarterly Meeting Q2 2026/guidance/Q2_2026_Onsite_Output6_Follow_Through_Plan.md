# Output 6: Post-Onsite Follow-Through Plan

The best onsite in the world is worthless if the outputs decay within two weeks. This plan is designed to be lightweight enough that it actually gets done, and specific enough that drift is visible.

---

## Principle

Follow-through should cost less than 30 minutes per week per leader. If it costs more, it won't happen. The mechanism should be self-reinforcing: doing the follow-through makes the leader's own work easier, not just satisfying a tracking requirement.

## Weekly (Ongoing)

**3-Bullet Metric Update**

Each leader sends a brief weekly update in a shared Teams channel or Confluence page. Format:

```
[Name] - Week of [date]
1. [Metric]: [current value] (target: [target], trend: [up/down/flat])
2. [Key progress item or blocker]
3. [One thing I need from this group]
```

Rules:
- No prose. Numbers and facts only.
- If you have nothing to report, say "no update" rather than writing filler.
- If your commitment from the onsite has a relevant data point, include it.
- Takes less than 5 minutes to write.

**Accountability Partner Check-In**

Each leader's assigned accountability partner checks in once per week for the first 30 days. Format: a brief message or DM asking "Did you do the thing?" The check-in is not a coaching session. It is a binary: did the commitment happen or not.

---

## Monthly (SEL Meeting Integration)

**Scorecard Review (15 min in one SEL meeting per month)**

- Review the team SMART goals from the onsite. For each: status, progress, blockers.
- Review the Continue/Start/Stop commitments. Are we actually continuing, starting, and stopping what we said?
- Quick check on any security/engineering or product/engineering alignment commitments.
- Update the Commitments Board status.

This replaces a separate follow-through meeting. It's integrated into an existing meeting cadence.

---

## Quarterly (Next Onsite)

**At the Q3 2026 onsite (or equivalent quarterly gathering):**

1. Open with the Q2 2026 commitments. Grade each one: achieved / partially achieved / not achieved.
2. Pull up each leader's personal vision and goals document. Ask: "How did you progress? What changed?"
3. Review the team SMART goals against outcomes. What moved? What didn't? Why?
4. Use the honest adoption check as the input for the next quarter's planning.

This creates a continuous loop: commit > track > review > recommit. Each onsite builds on the last rather than starting from zero.

## What to Do If Follow-Through Stalls

This is the most important section.

The Q425 onsite produced five artifacts with success criteria. The Q2 2026 onsite will assess honestly whether those were adopted. If the answer is "mostly not," then the follow-through mechanism itself needs to be simpler.

**If follow-through stalls in the first month:**
- Check whether the commitments were too ambitious. Reduce scope rather than abandon.
- Check whether the accountability partner mechanism is working. If not, switch to a brief facilitated round in the weekly SEL meeting.
- Check whether the tracking mechanism is too burdensome. If Confluence pages aren't getting updated, switch to a simple shared document or even a Teams message.

**If follow-through stalls after the first month:**
- Discuss openly in the next SEL meeting. "We committed to X at the onsite. We stopped doing it. Why?"
- This is not a failure of character. It is usually a failure of mechanism. Fix the mechanism.

**The minimum viable follow-through is:**
1. Commitments page exists and is accessible.
2. One 15-min monthly check in an existing meeting.
3. Accountability partners check in for 30 days.

Everything else is valuable but optional. Protect these three.

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- **Don't create a new meeting for follow-through.** Integrate into existing cadences.
- **Don't build elaborate tracking systems.** A shared page with names, commitments, and status is sufficient.
- **Don't rely on a single person (Guillermo) to chase everyone.** The accountability partner mechanism distributes this.
- **Don't wait until the next onsite to check.** Monthly is the minimum cadence. Weekly is better for the first 30 days.
- **Don't abandon commitments silently.** If something needs to change, name it and decide explicitly to change it. Silent abandonment is the death of onsite credibility.

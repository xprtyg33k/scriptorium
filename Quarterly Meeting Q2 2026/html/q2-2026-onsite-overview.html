The Theme

What “Sharpen the Standard” Means

Over the past two years, the Software Engineering Leadership team has done the work to define who it is, what it expects, and how it operates. Values are documented. An operating model exists. Roles are clearer. Expectations have been stated — in writing, in conversation, in performance reviews.

Q2 2026 is not about restarting. It is not about redefining. The identity work is done. The question now is whether the standard cuts — whether the commitments the team has made are producing measurable results in the hands of the people and teams it leads.

“We are not here to set a new direction. We are here to prove the direction is working — with evidence.”
From Narrative to Evidence

Every win is backed by data. Not “we improved deployment reliability” but “change failure rate moved from X% to Y% between November and March.” Claims require proof.

Specific Goals, Not Aspirations

Every goal has a number, an owner, and a deadline. Not “improve sprint predictability” but “sprint commitment accuracy above 80% by end of Q3, owned by a named leader, measured weekly.”

Commitments That Close

Root cause analyses get followed up. Artifacts from prior onsites get used or retired honestly. Commitments made in one quarter are checked in the next — by the whole team, not just the leader who made them.

Honesty Over Performance

If something is not working, the team names it plainly and diagnoses the pattern. Sharpening means precision, not perfection — specific enough to act on, honest enough to measure.

Strategic Context

Why This Theme, Why Now

Three converging factors make this the right focus for Q2 2026.

Driver 01

2026 OKRs Require Numbers, Not Direction

Experity’s 2026 goals are quantified and tied to revenue, retention, and customer outcomes. Engineering leadership cannot contribute to these by having good conversations. Measurable results is the ask.

Driver 02

Evidence-Based Leadership Is the Next Maturity Level

The team has grown from defining identity, to naming excellence, to building operating discipline. The next step is proving that leadership direction translates into outcomes teams and the org can see.

Driver 03

Q4 2025 Commitments Need Honest Review

The Q4 2025 onsite produced real artifacts: an operating model, a scorecard, a feedback framework, and AI experiment commitments. This onsite checks whether they are being used. That answer matters before building anything new.

Leadership Arc

Two Years of Intentional Development

Each quarterly onsite has built on the last. This is not a series of disconnected events — it is a deliberate arc of team maturity. The table below shows where the team has been and why Q2 2026 is the natural next step.

  • Q1 2024 Identity
    Who are we? First formal exercise as a leadership team. Documented values. Introduced OKRs and architecture thinking. The beginning of shared identity.
  • Q2 2024 Excellence
    What does good look like? Defined excellence for developers and leaders. Introduced outcomes vs. output. Named role clarity and the gap between the trivial many and the vital few.
  • Q3 2024 Focus
    What is the one thing? Prioritization as discipline. Introduced the Say/Do tracker, VSM opportunities, and “Disagree and Commit.” Each leader committed to one significant project.
  • Q1 2025 Growth
    What have you done to make others succeed? Pushed from definition into action. Introduced the Outward Mindset. Challenged each leader to articulate their unique contribution.
  • Q4 2025 Recalibration
    The most structured onsite to date. Built Operating Model v1.0. Created a three-tier scorecard. Adopted the GAIN feedback framework. Set five explicit success criteria for the quarter. Left with owned commitments.
  • Q2 2026 → Now
    Sharpen the Standard. The standard has been set. Now it must cut. This onsite asks: did the bar hold? Are the commitments producing evidence? Where is the gap between what we said and what we did — and what do we do about it?
Two-Day Focus

What the Team Is Working Through

The onsite is structured across two days. Monday belongs to the team: retrospective, behavioral accountability, and personal leadership growth. Tuesday integrates cross-functional partners and closes with commitments that carry forward into Q3.

👥

Tuesday guests: Dan (Director of Information Security) and Clarissa (Chief Product Officer) join Tuesday morning for structured alignment conversations — not as speakers, but as collaborators working through shared priorities with the engineering leadership team.

Day 1 — Monday

Evidence of Progress

Each leader presents wins and growth since the Q4 2025 onsite with supporting evidence. The bar: not claims, but proof. Where does the data exist, and where has it been missing?

Day 1 — Monday

Accountability to Q4 2025 Commitments

Honest review of every artifact from the Q4 onsite: Operating Model, Scorecard, GAIN framework, Architecture loop, AI experiments. What was adopted, what wasn’t, and why.

Day 1 — Monday

Leadership Behaviors: Continue / Start / Stop

The team evaluates its own operating habits, communication patterns, and accountability norms — not its teams’, its own. Top commitments are voted on and made visible for monthly tracking.

Day 1 — Monday

AI Adoption: From Experiments to Governance

AI tools are in use across teams but managed inconsistently. This session closes out the Q4 AI experiment commitments and establishes lightweight governance: approved tools, cost visibility, and learning-sharing mechanisms for Q3.

Day 1 — Monday

Tech Debt and Modernization

The team has over 2,500 story points of tech debt. Specific modernization work is in flight. This session surfaces honest progress, identifies the top three Q3 priorities, and checks whether the quarterly sprint cadence is functioning.

Day 2 — Tuesday

Security and Engineering Alignment

Dan (Information Security) and the engineering leaders work through the real tensions between security compliance requirements and engineering velocity — producing concrete joint commitments with owners on both sides.

Day 2 — Tuesday

Product Direction and Partnership

Clarissa (CPO) shares H2 2026 product direction and the team has an honest conversation about partnership quality: where engineering and product are aligned, where there is friction, and what to do about it.

Both Days

Personal Leadership Vision and Goals

Each leader drafts and then shares their personal vision for the remainder of 2026 — including where they are not yet the leader they want to be. Goals are specific, owned, and revisited monthly. The team witnesses and supports each other’s growth.

Day 2 — Tuesday

Team Goals: From Aspiration to Commitment

The team sets 8–10 goals for Q3 and H2 2026. Every surviving goal must answer “how would we know if we achieved this?” Goals that cannot are either refined or removed. What remains is what the team is held to.

Day 2 — Tuesday

Closing Commitments and Accountability

Each leader states one specific 30-day commitment, one behavior they will stop, and names an accountability partner. The follow-through cadence is confirmed. The onsite closes with ownership distributed across the team.

Expected Outcomes

What the Team Walks Away With

Every session is designed to produce an artifact or decision — not just discussion. These are the tangible outputs the onsite is accountable for.

  • Evidence-backed wins log. A documented record of what has measurably improved since Q4 2025 — by leader and by theme. Sets the bar for how wins are reported going forward.
  • Q4 2025 artifact adoption scorecard. An honest grade on each prior commitment: adopted, partially adopted, or not adopted — with root causes and decisions on what to carry forward.
  • Leadership behavior commitments board. The top behavioral changes the team votes to make, with owners — visible and referenced in monthly meetings.
  • AI governance decisions. Approved tools or criteria, cost guardrails, learning-sharing mechanism, and one new experiment per team for Q3.
  • Top 3 Q3 tech debt priorities. Named items, owners, and clarity on whether the quarterly sprint cadence is on track.
  • Security / engineering alignment commitments. 2–3 joint commitments with owners from both engineering and security that improve velocity without increasing risk.
  • Product / engineering alignment commitments. 2–3 partnership commitments from the conversation with the CPO, with shared understanding of H2 product direction.
  • Personal vision and goals documents. One per leader — specific, honest, and revisited at Q3. Not a performance artifact. A leadership development artifact.
  • 8–10 team SMART goals for Q3 and H2 2026. Each with a number, an owner, and a deadline. The team’s commitment artifact for the second half of the year.
  • Distributed accountability commitments. One 30-day action and one stop-doing per leader, each with a named peer accountability partner. Follow-through is the team’s responsibility, not any one person’s.

## Required exercises and agenda elements

These items must appear in the design in a coherent sequence.

### 1. Opening retrospective, round robin
Open with a retrospective focused on wins, progress, and evidence of growth since one of:
- the last onsite
- the start of 2026
- another appropriate reference point supported by the source material

Important:
- the visible agenda can frame this as wins, growth, and progress
- but the facilitation design must push people to **show evidence**
- avoid vague terms like "better" or "more efficient" unless backed by something concrete
- participants should be pushed toward measurable proof, examples, before/after comparison, reduced friction, better predictability, lower failure rate, faster recovery, improved clarity, stronger ownership, or other tangible indicators
- do not make this hidden evidence standard overly explicit on the participant-facing agenda

### 2. Continue / Start / Stop
Run a Continue / Start / Stop exercise after the retrospective.
It should be leadership-grade, not lightweight.
It must focus on:
- leader behaviors
- operating habits
- communication patterns
- prioritization
- focus protection
- modernization behaviors
- accountability norms
- AI adoption habits where relevant
- architectural and delivery discipline

### 3. Personal vision and goals round robin
Each leader should articulate:
- personal vision for the remainder of 2026
- goals for the next quarter, up to the next onsite
- how that vision reflects character and core values
- how it aligns with and contributes to collective goals
- where there may be gaps between intent and current behavior

This must produce a documented artifact that can be revisited later.

### 4. Whiteboard goals exercise
Run a whiteboard exercise where participants generate:
- goals
- aspirations
- objectives
- outcomes they want the team and organization to achieve

Then have that exercise naturally convert those into SMART goals.
Important:
- this conversion should be **designed into the facilitation**
- but the participant-facing agenda should not make the "SMART conversion" feel like a contrived trick
- it should feel like a natural refinement from aspiration into measurable commitment

### 5. Team-building / outdoor mental reset
Include a practical, low-cheese team-building or outdoor reset exercise titled or themed around:
- Carry the Load
- Team First

This should not feel like camp or forced bonding.
It should support:
- trust
- shared burden
- leadership responsibility
- resilience
- mental reset
- reflection on collective load and leadership posture

## Agenda design principles

Design the onsite around these principles:

### 1. Put the hardest thinking early
Use the highest energy time for:
- retrospective with evidence
- operating model discussion
- accountability / maturity
- priorities
- metrics
- decision-making
- future state alignment

### 2. Protect the afternoons
Afternoons should still matter, but they should be:
- more interactive
- lower cognitive load
- more reflective
- more collaborative
- less lecture-based
- less abstract

### 3. Use guests intentionally
Dan and Clarissa should not be decorative guest speakers.
Design their participation so it contributes to:
- alignment
- real discussion
- challenge
- clarity
- stronger shared context

Also propose a backup structure in case one or both schedules shift.

### 4. Every session should earn its place
Each session in the agenda must have:
- objective
- why it matters now
- output or artifact
- facilitation format
- ideal length
- energy profile
- dependencies on earlier sessions, if any

### 5. Keep it practical
This is a leadership team.
Do not fill the agenda with broad, generic leadership content.
Everything should tie back to:
- how leaders operate
- how the team functions
- how engineering matures
- how work gets prioritized and measured
- how excellence is reinforced
- how leaders create clarity and accountability

### 6. Bias toward artifacts
Prefer sessions that produce something tangible:
- measurable win examples
- continue/start/stop outputs
- explicit team commitments
- personal vision statements
- SMART goals
- operating norms
- accountability statements
- metrics shortlist
- next-quarter commitments
- decisions about AI experimentation or governance
- identified cross-team impediments
- a leadership charter or refinement, if justified by the analysis

9:30 – 10:15

Blind Read — Engineering Reviews Artifact Pack Cold
45 min · Isaac & Andrew read · Product observes silently
No Context Assist Critical
This is the most important 45 minutes of the two-day exercise. Isaac and Andrew read the full artifact pack in silence. Product and Product-side participants observe but do not speak. Every question Engineering has during this read is a signal about whether the artifact is sufficient for autonomous action. The facilitator captures first-impressions in real time.

📦 Artifact Pack — Reading Order

  • 1. Product Outcome Brief — Problem statement, target user, business outcome, success metric.
  • 2. MVP / PRD Brief — Scope boundary, explicit exclusions, assumptions, open questions, acceptance signals.
  • 3. Workflow & Context Brief — Current flow, desired flow, systems involved, known dependencies.
  • 4. Candidate Execution Slices — Outcome statements, validation gates, sequencing rationale.
  • 5. Readiness & Validation Log — 9 open questions with owners, Day 1 decisions, named risks.

📝 Facilitator: What to Capture During the Read

  • Every time a validator pauses and re-reads a section — mark it as a potential ambiguity.
  • Any facial or verbal reaction (including remote video) — note the section being read.
  • Any questions asked aloud (even if premature) — capture verbatim, respond with "hold that for S2."
  • Which sections were read quickly vs. slowly — pace is a signal of clarity or density.
  • Time each artifact section — flag any section that takes more than 10 minutes for a single reader.

⚠️ Facilitator Watch-Outs During Blind Read

  • Do not explain or clarify anything unprompted — silence from Product is the discipline being tested.
  • If a validator directly asks a question, respond only with "Hold that — we'll capture it in the Q&A."
  • If Andrew (remote) appears lost, ask "Are you on the right section?" — connectivity, not content, is fair to assist with.
  • Do not let side conversations start between Product participants — the read is a signal-gathering period.

✅ Exit Criteria

  • Both Isaac and Andrew have completed reading all 5 artifacts
  • First-impression questions captured (even if unanswered)
  • Sections that caused hesitation or re-reads are marked
  • No verbal context was provided by Product during the read

Validation Round 1 — First Impressions & Scope Questions
60 min · Engineering leads · Product answers only what's asked
Engineering-Led Q&A
Engineering surfaces every question, gap, and ambiguity from the blind read. This is a structured Q&A — not a requirements refinement session. Product answers questions concisely and documents any answer that is not already in the artifact as an artifact gap. The facilitator drives the structured validation prompts if the conversation stalls.

🔎 Structured Validation Prompts (Guillermo)

  • "Without any additional context, can you describe what problem this product is solving and for whom?"
  • "Is the MVP scope boundary clear enough to start work? What would you need to know before writing the first line of code?"
  • "What questions does the Workflow & Context Brief leave unanswered? Which integration dependencies are underspecified?"
  • "Do the Execution Slices make sense to you as an engineering team? Is Slice 1 a viable starting point?"
  • "What is missing from the artifact pack that, if absent in Sprint 1, would cause a blocker?"

📋 Feedback Capture Framework

  • Understandable: Can Engineering explain what is being built, for whom, and why without help?
  • Actionable: Is the MVP scope defined precisely enough to start Sprint 1 planning?
  • Sufficient Integration Context: Are the systems, dependencies, and APIs documented at an actionable level?
  • Slice Viability: Are the execution slices outcome-framed, correctly sequenced, and realistic?
  • Missing Context: What is Engineering blocked by that isn't in the packet?

⚡ Day 1 Named Risks — Probe These Specifically

  • R1: Care Agent skill dependency APIs (carrier event schema) — does Engineering know what's missing and can they specify it?
  • R2: Slice 1 validation gate — can Engineering propose a binary, observable gate criterion?
  • R3: Patient-facing experience — Engineering should confirm whether this is in or out of Slice 1.
  • R4: Workflow diagram (whiteboard photo) — is it in the artifact? If not, does Engineering need it to proceed?
  • R5: Systems inventory completeness — can Engineering identify what's missing and prioritize it?

🚩 What Passes vs. Fails This Session

  • Pass: Engineering can re-state the problem and goal without Product prompting. → Artifact is sufficient.
  • Probe: Engineering can describe scope but has 1–3 clarification questions. → Artifact needs minor additions.
  • Fail: Engineering cannot describe what they would build from the artifacts alone. → Artifact gaps are critical.
  • All three outcomes are acceptable today — "fail" produces the most valuable feedback for the next pod's Day 1.

🔌 Integration Deep Dive Prompts

🧩 Architecture Context Check

  • Does the artifact describe where this code lives in the system architecture? (Scribe service? New microservice? Skill layer?)

  • Are there security or compliance constraints (HIPAA, PHI handling) that affect the integration design?
  • Does Andrew's video wrapper POC need to be documented as a technical dependency input — and is it currently described in the artifact?

  • If Engineering says "I'd need the event schema before we start" — that is a Sprint 1 kickoff prerequisite, not a sprint discovery item.

Readiness Scoring — Engineering Scores the Artifact Pack
45 min · Engineering scores each dimension · Product captures gaps
Scoring Structured
Engineering scores the artifact pack across five dimensions on a 1–5 scale. This structured scoring produces a traceable, objective readiness assessment that becomes the input for the Go / No-Go decision in S6. Each score requires a brief rationale — not a number alone.

📊 Scoring Dimensions (1 = Not ready · 5 = Sprint-ready)

  • 1. Problem Clarity — Can Engineering describe the problem and target user from the artifact alone?
  • 2. Scope Definition — Is the MVP boundary explicit and unambiguous? Are exclusions clear?
  • 3. Integration Context — Are the system interactions, dependencies, and API expectations documented at an actionable level?
  • 4. Slice Viability — Are the execution slices correctly outcome-framed, sequenced, and sized for Sprint 1?
  • 5. Agent-Readiness — Could a well-configured AI coding agent start on Slice 1 using only these artifacts as context?

🎯 Scoring Standard

  • 5 — Sprint-ready: Engineering would start tomorrow without additional context.
  • 4 — Ready with minor clarifications: 1–2 quick answers needed, then start.
  • 3 — Proceed with named gaps: Key items missing but workarounds exist for Sprint 1 start.
  • 2 — Significant rework needed: Multiple critical gaps; Sprint 1 start would be high-risk.
  • 1 — Not actionable: Cannot start; artifacts need a Day 1 redo for this section.
  • Every score below 4 must have a specific gap description — not a vague statement.

2:00 – 3:15
🛠️
Gap Resolution Working Session
75 min · All participants · Close every critical gap or assign an owner
Working Session Critical Path
Every critical gap identified in S2–S4 gets one of three resolutions in this session: Answer now (Product or Engineering provides the answer and the artifact is updated live), Defer with rationale (explicitly decided, owner named, date set), or Escalate (requires input from someone not in the room — owner assigned, timeline committed). No gap leaves this session as "we'll figure it out."

🔧 Resolution Protocol

  • Read each critical gap from the S4 list.
  • Can we answer this right now with the people in this room? → If yes: answer it, update the artifact, mark closed.
  • Is this a decision that needs a stakeholder not present? → Escalate: name the owner, commit to a date before Sprint 1 kickoff.
  • Is this gap acceptable if we explicitly acknowledge the risk? → Defer: write the rationale, name the risk, add to the Readiness Log.
  • Lauren updates artifacts in real time as answers are captured — not post-session.

  • OQ-2 (P1): Slice 1 binary validation gate — can Isaac and Product define the observable event together now?
  • OQ-3 (P1): Patient-facing scope — can we close this with a definitive yes/no from the Product Owner?
  • OQ-4 (P1): Contractor onboarding — can Guillermo confirm the handoff plan now?
  • OQ-5 (P1): Workflow diagram in shared folder — Lauren confirms embed status and fixes it live if not done.

📝 Artifact Update Protocol (Lauren)

  • For each closed gap: update the relevant artifact section live during the session.
  • For each escalated gap: add to the Readiness Log with owner and date.
  • For each deferred gap: add the explicit rationale to the exclusions or assumptions section.
  • After this session, re-run the blind-read check on any section that was updated — does it still pass?

🚫 What This Session Is NOT

  • Not a re-scope of the MVP — scope was set on Day 1 and validated in S2.
  • Not an architecture design session — Engineering can note architectural questions for Sprint 1 planning, not resolve them here.
  • Not a blame session — gaps in the artifact are process improvements for the next pod, not performance issues.
  • Not optional — every critical gap gets a resolution before S6. No exceptions.

Go / No-Go — Sprint 1 Kickoff Decision
45 min · Guillermo facilitates · Engineering and Product decide together
Gate Decision
The Go / No-Go decision is a formal milestone in the AI-Native PDLC. We are deciding whether the combined artifact pack — including the gaps resolved in S5 — provides sufficient clarity to begin Sprint 1. A "No-Go" is not a failure. It means we found the gaps before writing code, which is exactly the point of the exercise.

🚦 Go / No-Go Options

  • Go — Artifact pack is actionable. Sprint 1 kickoff can proceed as planned. All critical gaps are closed or have committed owners and dates.
  • Go with Pre-Conditions — Sprint 1 can start, but specific pre-conditions must be met first (named gaps with resolution deadline before Day 1 of Sprint 1).
  • No-Go — Critical gaps remain that would stall Sprint 1 within the first week. The artifacts need refinement before kickoff. Sprint 1 date moves or scope shrinks.

🔎 Go / No-Go Check Questions

  • Can Engineering begin Sprint 1 planning without additional Product availability?
  • Is the Slice 1 outcome statement and validation gate specific enough to plan against?
  • Are the integration pre-requisites documented with owners and committed dates?
  • Would an AI coding agent loaded with these artifacts produce useful first-draft code for Slice 1?
  • Is the contractor onboarding plan clear — will new resources have what they need on day one?

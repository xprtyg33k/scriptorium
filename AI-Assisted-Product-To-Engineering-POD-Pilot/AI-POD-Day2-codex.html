Meeting Intent

Day 2 is the validation loop for the AI-native product process. The goal is to prove whether better product context creates faster, cleaner engineering action, not to run a traditional architecture design session.

Anchor: AI Acceleration Strategy

Read Before Reacting

Reviewers consume the full packet before raising questions. The test is whether the artifacts answer questions without live narration.

Find The Failure Mode

Every concern is categorized as ambiguity, missing context, over-prescription, future-phase runway, technical translation, or validation gap.

Leave With A Pattern

The team should end with a repeatable handoff standard, not just a better telehealth conversation.

AI-Native Validation Model

Use the same strategy frame as Day 1, but turn each stage into a validation question.

Signal Does the packet preserve the customer, market, workflow, and business signals that led to telehealth?
Context Can reviewers understand scope, dependencies, product boundaries, and source material without a narrated tour?
Decision Are the decisions clear enough to move, and are unresolved questions tagged with owners and impact?
Execution Can the packet produce TRD direction, work items, spikes, QA criteria, and coding-agent prompts?
Memory Will the final artifacts, decisions, and lessons be findable and reusable for the next initiative?

Day 2 Agenda

Each block is designed to create observable evidence about the quality of the handoff. Use the accordions to guide the discussion and capture notes during the session.

Validation Decision Board

Use this board before closing. Each cell should end as green, yellow, or a named action with an owner.

Understandable

Can reviewers explain the problem, users, scope, and success measures in their own words?

  • Problem and goal are clear.
  • Module boundaries are clear.
  • Future phases do not blur MVP.

Actionable

Can the packet generate work without Product narrating missing requirements?

  • Epics, stories, spikes, and QA checks can be drafted.
  • Open questions are named and owned.
  • Acceptance criteria are testable.

Translatable

Can Engineering and Architecture turn the packet into a TRD without Product deciding the how?

  • Architecture standards can be applied.
  • Events and metrics are product-owned.
  • Technical decisions remain in the technical lane.

Repeatable

Can the team reproduce this workflow on the next initiative?

  • Inputs are known.
  • Templates are usable.
  • Shared memory location is settled.

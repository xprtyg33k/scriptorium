PRDs Strong Met

PRD And Product Definition Drafting

The working session exposed exactly the right PRD correction: product requirements were useful, but some were too implementation-prescriptive.

  • Telehealth Connect focused on patient readiness: identity, state attestation, gates, AV check, and ready signal.
  • AI Scribe focused on provider workflow: queue visibility, video session, audio capture, documentation, and encounter writeback.
  • The team aligned on outcome language: Product should specify the needed communication, events, and acceptance criteria, not endpoints, tables, or Kafka details.
Ownership Strong Met

Ownership Lanes And Guardrails

The clearest operating agreement of the day was that Product owns the what and why, including required business events and metrics, while Engineering and Architecture own the how through a TRD.

  • Events became first-class product requirements rather than after-the-fact telemetry.
  • TRD generation was described as PRD plus architecture standards plus existing system context.
  • QA validation should come from product acceptance criteria, not ad hoc interpretation.

Why It Matters

Disposition

Day 2 Readiness Questions

Day 2 should not ask Engineering whether they like the documents. It should ask whether they can act on them, what they would generate from them, and where the packet fails under real delivery pressure.

Understandability

After a silent read, can reviewers explain the problem, users, scope, non-goals, success measures, and major dependencies without a verbal walkthrough?

Actionability

Can the packet generate usable epics, stories, spikes, QA criteria, or agent prompts without Product filling in missing context live?

Technical Translation

Can Engineering combine the PRD, architecture standards, and existing context into a credible TRD without Product specifying tables, endpoints, or transport?

Telemetry

Are events and metrics clear enough to support operational monitoring, reporting, usage analytics, and product learning?

Scope Discipline

Do future-phase requirements preserve runway without bloating the MVP or disguising critical constraints as vague later work?

Repeatability

Can the team name the process used to create the packet, reproduce it for another initiative, and improve it after the validation?

Recommended Day 2 Opening Message

Frame

This is a validation of the handoff, not a defense of the handoff. Read the packet first, then tell us where it enables action and where it still asks Engineering to guess.

Standard

Feedback should map to a specific artifact, section, missing decision, or unclear requirement. The output is an improved process and a better telehealth delivery starting point.

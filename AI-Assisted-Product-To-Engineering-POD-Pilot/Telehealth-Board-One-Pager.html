📈 Strategic Value Proposition

Market expansion — Enables Experity clients to offer virtual visits without third-party telehealth vendors, keeping patients and revenue inside the urgent care ecosystem.
AI-native differentiation — AI Scribe and Care Agent deliver automated documentation and patient orchestration that competitors cannot replicate without deep EMR integration.
Operational efficiency — Reduces manual coordination between intake, scheduling, charting, and billing by unifying the telehealth workflow inside the existing platform.
Revenue opportunity — Opens new visit volume for clients (after-hours, overflow, follow-up) and creates a platform-sticky service with recurring SaaS economics.

Key Risks & Dependencies

Cross-Product Coordination

Five workstreams touch separate product and engineering teams. Sequencing and shared interfaces require intentional orchestration.

External Dependencies

Video infrastructure vendor selection, carrier integrations, and regulatory compliance (state licensing, consent) carry timeline risk.

AI Model Readiness

AI Scribe and Care Agent extensions require new prompt engineering, telehealth-specific training data, and quality validation before clinical use.

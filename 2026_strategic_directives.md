🔺 Strategic Directives to Influence Board Evaluation
1. AI Is Not Experimental—It's a Strategic Pillar

Big Bets:

AI in EMR (double down)

AI in RCM (automation as a product)

AI for patient engagement (Care Agent skill expansion)

Implication: Initiatives involving AI (especially for EMR, RCM, or patient experience) should be evaluated as high-leverage bets, not speculative tech plays.

2. RCM as a Product

Reframing RCM from service to productized automation, especially as margin pressure and churn risk escalate.

Implication: Proposals involving RCM improvements or automation should emphasize product leverage, cost-to-serve reduction, and client retention outcomes.

3. Platform and Marketplace Shift

OnePACS and other solutions are being pushed to evolve from “tools” to platforms that enable ecosystem value (e.g., teleradiology marketplace, bundled compliance).

Implication: Initiatives that support neutral, multi-tenant SaaS platforms should be evaluated with higher architectural tolerance if they promise long-term leverage.

4. Customer Experience as Churn Armor

Detractor data centers on support responsiveness, feature parity, integration depth, and proactive communication.

Implication: Proposals that improve integration, roadmap transparency, support NPS, or reduce release frictions should be prioritized—even if the ROI is indirect.

5. Leadership-Driven Accountability

Each offsite leader will personally commit to execution areas tied to 2026 goals.

Scorecard expectations include "From X to Y" measurable targets.

Implication: Initiatives submitted for board review should now include explicit owner accountability, success deltas, and integration into the 2026 VSKI or priority map.
